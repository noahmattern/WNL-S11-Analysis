World Ninja League · Season XI · Tier 1 Championships

Where the runs ended

Fail distribution across 16 divisions and three stages, from 4,395 championship runs. On these courses a single miss ends the run, so each athlete's result marks exactly one obstacle — the one that stopped them — or a finish.

Stage 1

48.3%of 2,138 starters finished

The Octopus, the final obstacle, ended 32% of all runs — more than the other seven obstacles combined. The outlier is Preteen Male, where Spinning Baskets alone eliminated 39% of the division.

Stage 2

17.0%of 1,326 starters finished

Salmon Ladder to Sky Trap was the wall for the field at large, taking 37% of all starters. The Katanas told a different story: only a third of the field ever reached them, but they ended most Elite runs — Elite Male finished the stage zero times.

Stage 3

1.8%of 931 starters finished

One executioner: Swivel Limit eliminated 53% of everyone who started the stage, reaching 60–72% in several divisions. Only 17 athletes cleared the Flying Bar to finish.

Method. Results scraped from the official WNL T1 Championships results (NinjaWorks timing data). Because a fail ends the run, an athlete's fail point is one past their last completed obstacle; obstacle names and order were reconstructed from the recorded results and the published course maps. Heatmap cells show the percentage of division starters eliminated at each obstacle; · means none; values under 1% display as <1.
